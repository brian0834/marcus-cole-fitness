Nutrition Guide

What To Eat

No tracking apps required. These are clear targets and rules that work for most body types. Marcus will adjust your specific numbers on your first check-in.

1g
Protein / lb bodyweight
Non-negotiable. Spread across 4–5 meals. Chicken, beef, eggs, fish, Greek yogurt.
40%
Carbs (of total calories)
Time around training. Oats, rice, sweet potato, fruit. Cut back on rest days.
25%
Fats (of total calories)
Avocado, olive oil, nuts, eggs. Never go below 50g — hormones depend on it.
Sample Daily Meal Plan
7AM
Breakfast — Pre-Training
5 eggs scrambled + 1 cup oats with banana and almond butter
Black coffee or green tea. No sugar.
10AM
Pre-Workout Snack
1 cup Greek yogurt (plain, full-fat) + handful of blueberries + rice cake
Take this 60–90 min before training.
1PM
Post-Workout Meal
200g grilled chicken breast + 1.5 cups white rice + steamed broccoli
Eat within 60 min of finishing your session.
4PM
Afternoon Meal
Salmon (150–200g) or lean ground beef + sweet potato + mixed greens with olive oil
Largest carb window of the day after this point narrows.
7PM
Dinner
Steak or chicken thighs + roasted vegetables + 1 cup cottage cheese or eggs
Lower carbs here unless training in the evening.
9PM
Optional Nighttime
Casein protein shake or 200g cottage cheese + handful of nuts
Slow-digesting protein feeds muscle repair overnight.

Non-Negotiable Rules
01
Hit your protein every day. Everything else is secondary. If you only do one thing right, make it this. 1g per lb of bodyweight, minimum.
02
Drink at least 3 litres of water daily. More on training days. Dehydration tanks performance and recovery faster than almost anything else.
03
Sleep 7–9 hours. This is not optional. No supplement, no training method, and no amount of protein compensates for poor sleep. Your body builds muscle while you sleep.
04
Eat within 60 minutes after training. Post-workout is your highest insulin sensitivity window. Use it — protein and carbs here go directly toward recovery.
05
Don't skip meals to "save calories." Undereating stalls progress and leads to muscle loss. Eat your full targets every day, including rest days.
06
Keep a training log. Write down every weight, every set, every rep. If you're not tracking, you're guessing. Progress is a pattern — you can't see the pattern without data.
07
Message Marcus after every session. One line — what you lifted, how it felt. He adjusts your plan based on this. The feedback loop is the program.
